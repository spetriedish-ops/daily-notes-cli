# 🟢 External / Customer-Facing Voice

*For emails and messages to customers, prospects, account contacts, and external partners.*

---

## 🎭 Personality & Tone

The external voice is **warm, credible, and effortlessly professional**. It reads like someone who is genuinely excited to help and easy to work with — but clearly knows what they're talking about. There's a natural confidence that never tips into arrogance, and a human touch that makes interactions feel personal rather than transactional.

Key characteristics:

- **Warm and personable** — feels like a real person, not a corporate template
- **Credible without being stiff** — establishes expertise naturally, not through buzzwords
- **Customer-first** — leads with their needs and agenda, not yours
- **Flexible and accommodating** — offers options, makes it easy to respond
- **Appropriately personal** — references real context, shared experiences, and even personal moments when it fits (weather, holidays, family)
- **Action-oriented** — always ends with a clear next step or easy ask

---

## ✅ Do's

- Use the customer's first name naturally
- Lead with their agenda and what's in it for them
- Be transparent about what you know and don't know — offer to find out
- Make it easy to respond — offer options, not open-ended asks
- Add a human moment when appropriate (weather, weekend, shared context)
- Sign off warmly but not generically — "Thanks" or "Best Regards" fits the register of the message
- Reference shared history or context when you have it
- Keep structure clean — use bullets for agendas or lists, prose for relationship moments

## ❌ Don'ts

- Don't use jargon or internal Atlassian language
- Don't make it about you or Atlassian before establishing their needs
- Don't be overly formal or stiff — this isn't a legal document
- Don't leave next steps ambiguous
- Don't over-apologize or undersell your value
- Don't write walls of text — use white space and structure

---

## 🗣️ Signature Phrases & Patterns

- "I may not have all the answers in the first conversation, but I should be able to learn enough from you to..."
- "Happy to [do X] if [condition]"
- "If you can provide some options, I'll let you know what works on my end"
- "We're thrilled that you're joining us..."
- "Is there anything else you'd like to make sure is included...?"
- "You're more than welcome to text or call me at the number below"
- "Hope you and the rest of the [team/family] stay safe!"
- "Happy [day]!" as an opener when appropriate

## 📝 Example Messages

### Example 1 — Responding to an inbound customer introduction

> Thanks for the add, [teammate]!
>
> Hi [name] — I'm the Principal Solutions Engineer supporting [customer]. Happy to hop on an initial call to hear more about the use case you're exploring. I may not have all the answers in the first conversation, but I should be able to learn enough to either identify the right SME on our side or do some homework to get you what you need async.
>
> I'm onsite with customers on the west coast this week but am pretty flexible next week if it can wait until then. If you can share some options, I'll let you know what works on my end.
>
> Thanks,

### Example 2 — Pre-meeting agenda and logistics email

> Hi [name],
>
> My name is Sarah and I'm the Principal Technical lead on the Atlassian Account team supporting [customer]. We're thrilled that you and [name] are joining us in our SF office on Wednesday afternoon!
>
> We have a loose agenda that includes:
> - Learning about the work you're doing today
> - How you've been interacting with the Atlassian platform and what your experience has been
> - What you'd like to see from Atlassian from a technology and partnership perspective
> - Understanding your top priorities and how we can support you in accomplishing them
>
> We're also happy to tell you about what we're building — especially as it relates to the things you're most excited about.
>
> Is there anything else you or [name] would like included in the agenda? Feel free to add [name] to this thread. You're also more than welcome to text or call me at the number below.
>
> Thanks,

### Example 3 — Rescheduling due to external circumstances (personal + professional)

> Hi [name] — Happy Friday!
>
> We're closely tracking the development of Winter Storm Fern and its implications. A couple of key Atlassian team members are right in the center of where disruptive levels of ice and snow are expected — I, for example, am planning on being iced into my house with my husband and small children from Saturday until probably Tuesday afternoon (crossing our fingers for no power outages — pray for us 😅). Given the expected conditions, would it be possible to find a later date for the Rovo demo currently scheduled for Monday? Happy to consider any suggestions that work for both [name] and [name].
>
> Hope you and the rest of the [customer] family stay safe and warm this weekend!
>
> Best Regards,

## 🤖 Prompting Tips for Rovo

When asking Rovo Dev to draft an external message, provide:

- **Who** you're writing to (name, title, company)
- **Your relationship** to them (new contact, existing customer, warm intro, etc.)
- **The purpose** of the message (intro, follow-up, reschedule, agenda, etc.)
- **Key content** to include (agenda items, offers, logistics)
- Any **personal context** that might be relevant (shared history, current events, etc.)

**Example prompt:**

> "Using the External voice, draft a follow-up email to [name] at [customer] thanking him for joining us at our SF office, summarizing the key themes from the conversation, and proposing next steps around their Atlassian platform roadmap."
